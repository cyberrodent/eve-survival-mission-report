Part 1 of 3


Faction: Amarr
Mission type: Encounter
Space type: Deadspace, fixed warp-in point
Damage dealt: Therm/EM
Extras: Neut, Tracking Disrupt,
Web/scramble: Web/Scram
Recommended damage dealing: EM, Therm
Recommended ships classes: Battlecruiser
Recommended generic setup: Passive tank and medium weapons.

Single spawn (35km)


2x Elite Frigates (Divine Imperial Imran) Scramble
3x Elite Frigates (Divine Imperial Napat) Web
3x Elite Cruisers (Divine Imperial Ambrose) Neut
2x Elite Cruisers (Divine Imperial Justicar) Painter
2x Battlecruisers (Imperial Templar Champion)
1x Cruisers (Imperial Templar Seer) Neut
4x Tower Sentry Amarr III

Mission completion upon destruction of Broadcasting Array.

Standings: 2.4% loss for destroying the Broadcasting Array

Tags: 1x Imperial Navy Major I
2x Imperial Navy Sergeant II
3x Imperial Navy Sergeant III
17x Imperial Navy Colonel I

Faction: Angel Cartel / Amarr

Extras: None
Web/scramble: None
Recommended damage dealing: Explosive/EM
Recommended ships classes: Battleship
Recommended generic setup: No recommendation

2x Battleships (Gist War General)

The first part of the mission HAS scramblers. The elite frigates fall into 2 groups: 2 target disruptors and 4 webbers out of which, 2 also warpscramble. Was a second too late and got webbed and neuted so fast, lost my BS in less than 2 minutes after warping in. If you value your ship, go in with a passive BC or bring some DPS to nuke the array and get out in 20-30s tops. Good drone skills are also a must as once they get their neut on you, your cap goes down real fast.

-- BugaBuga (2009-01-01 19:06:49)
For Part 1 I decided to ignore above comments and went in in an active tanked Golem. My plan was to blitz it. However I started aligning for warp almost immediately and this moved me towards the group, and 2 seconds before the array popped I was scrambled. It was no problem at all taking everything out, but keep in mind my skills are pretty much maxed (43mill SP atm), including my TP, shield boost, cap recharge and capacity, shield recharge and capacity, drones, etc.
My cap did not drop below 66% while keeping the shield above 50%. Did have a cap recharger II, heavy NOS and drones that took out the scramblers first just in case.
So 2 comments here:
1. I think so long as you go in with enough cap recharge, and have the damage to take out the smaller targets (ie. Target painter and drones), this mission is no probs. Damage was about medium (ie. quite high for such small ships).
2. If you plan to Blitz move away from the structure while shooting it (depending on your damage possibly with an AB) to avoid being scrambled before its destroyed.
Also one of the cruisers dropped a medium unstable power fluctuator which was worth about 12mill in the region I was in.

-- ForteEXE (2009-12-03 21:39:58)
Part 1 is gate-less deadspace. It can therefore be sniped.

Warp in with a shuttle, drop a bookmark. Warp back to station. You can test the bookmark by warping to the bookmark within 100K. Repeat the process to get even a larger distance. When you're confident you have enough distance, get your sniper ship. Kill frigates first, then the cruised-sized ships at leasure.

2 Phased Weapon Navigation arrays and precision cruise missiles made minced-meat out of all the rats.

At 100K+, enemy DPS comes from the Tower Sentries. Using Beam Lasers, they have a very long Optimum Range.

Done in a cap-stable Golem, 4 Cruise Luanchers II, X-Large Shield Booster, 1 Heat and 1 EM Hardener tech II, 2 Phased Weapon Navigation Array, 2 Cap Booster II's, 4 Ballistic Controls II, 2 CCC Rigs.

None of the rats came in range to do any damage or their EW on me.
